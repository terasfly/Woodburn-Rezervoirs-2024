History

During the 19th and early 20th centuries, Woodburn was part of a thriving industrial landscape. The reservoir itself was constructed in the late 1800s to provide water for the growing population of Carrickfergus and the surrounding area. During World War II, Woodburn Forest was used by the military for training exercises and as a storage area for ammunition and supplies.In more recent years, Woodburn Forest has become a popular destination for outdoor recreation, offering opportunities for hiking, cycling, fishing, and other activities.

Woodburn Forest is situated on the Antrim Plateau, an area shaped by glacial activity during the last Ice Age. The landscape features rolling hills, rocky outcrops, and dense woodland. The forest is home to a variety of plant and animal species, including native trees like oak, ash, and birch, as well as wildlife such as red squirrels, foxes, and birds of prey.
